Chicken Tikka Shawarma üçõ

Description

This recipe is a combination of the ever-popular shawarma hailing from the Middle East and the delicious Chicken Tikka Masala from India. From my personal experience, shawarmas are typically a bit dry, spiceless, and sometimes lacking flavor, while Chicken Tikka Masala seems to deliver so much flavor that you can get tired of it after a bit. The rich spices and creaminess of the masala will work to balance out the dryness of the shawarma, and vice versa. It's a match made in heaven!

Ingredients

For Chicken

Marinade

  • 28 oz Boneless Skinless Chicken Breast, chopped up into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tsp Garam Masala
  • 1 tsp ground Cumin
  • 1.5 tbsp minced garlic
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tbsp ginger (can use paste)
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder (or a different chili powder)
  • 1 tsp Paprika (optional)
  • 1 tsp salt

Sauce

  • Cooking oil of choice
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 1.25 cups heavy or thickened cream
  • 14 oz tomato puree (or tomatoes to blend in the cream)
  • 1.5 tbsp garlic, finely grated or minced
  • Same amounts of the spices listed in the "Marinade" Section
  • Cilantro/coriander for garnish

For Shawarma

  • Pita bread
  • Tahini Sauce or:
  • 0.5 cup Tahini paste + 1 tbsp olive oil + 0.5 lemon, juiced + salt
  • Olives, crushed (optional)
  • Peppers (optional)

Steps

  1. Combine all marinade ingredients with the chicken in a ziploc bag.
  2. Store in fridge for up to 48 hrs; the longer you marinate, the better! If you can only do an hour or two, make some light cuts in the chicken so it can absorb everything better.
  3. After taking the chicken out of the fridge, it's time for the sauce. Start with a pan or skillet on medium heat.
  4. Once the pan is hot, toss in some cooking oil of choice or butter.
  5. Throw in butter and onions; let cook until golden (3-4 mins).
  6. Toss in ginger and garlic, let cook for 30 seconds. Be sure to stir to prevent burning.
  7. Add in marinated chicken, tomato puree, and all spices from the marinade section in same quantities. Use more butter if desired. Stir well to mix and cook over medium-high heat for 5-6 minutes. (Note: if using tomatoes, blend them with some cream first to make a puree - this is necessary for good masala.)
  8. Add in your heavy cream and simmer for 8-10 mins, stirring occasionally throughout.
  9. While the chicken tikka masala is simmering, start preparing your shawarma wrap - olive oil on a pan over medium heat, put on pita bread (2-3 minutes each side or to preference).
  10. If you don't have tahini sauce, combine tahini paste, lemon juice, and olive oil in a bowl. Spread this on your toasted pita bread.
  11. Put on some crushed olives and peppers if desired.
  12. Put your chicken tikka masala onto the pita bread, wrap it up, and voila! A good tip is to not put too much of the masala sauce in the wraps themselves, and instead leave some to dip the wraps in while eating. Enjoy!
